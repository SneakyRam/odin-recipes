BIRIYANI



Biryani: A Dish of Flavor and Tradition

Biryani is a popular South Asian dish of flavored rice, meat, vegetables, or eggs cooked with aromatic spices in a layered method. Originating from Persian cuisine, it is prepared with fragrant long-grain rice, such as basmati, and various spices, often slow-cooked in a sealed pot using the Dum Pukht technique to lock in flavor and moisture.


Ingredients:


  • 1 ½ - 2 cups aged basmati rice
  • Water for soaking and cooking
  • Whole spices: 1 bay leaf, 4 green cardamoms, 6 cloves, 1 inch cinnamon stick, 1 star anise
  • ½ kg chicken
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup yogurt (curd)
  • ½ to 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ to 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 tbsp oil or ghee
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 1 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • ½ tsp saffron strands soaked in 3 tbsp hot milk
  • Fried onions (store-bought or homemade) for garnish
  • 1 tbsp biryani masala (optional)

Steps:

  1. Prepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 30 minutesPrepare the Rice: Rinse the basmati rice thoroughly and soak it in water for at least 30 minutes
  2. Marinate the Chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, salt, and lemon juice. Marinate for at least 1 hour
  3. Sauté Onions: Heat oil or ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add the sliced onions and fry them on medium heat until they turn golden brown
  4. Cook the Chicken: Add the marinated chicken to the pot and sauté until it becomes pale, about 5 minutes. You can also add biryani masala at this stage.
  5. Layer the Biryani
  6. Dum Cook: Sprinkle the saffron-infused milk over the top layer of rice. Cover the pot tightly with a lid, ensuring no steam escapes (a tea towel can be placed under the lid)
  7. Rest and Serve: Cook on high heat for 3 minutes, then reduce the heat to low and cook for another 10 minutes. Switch off the heat and let the biryani rest for 10-20 minutes before gently mixing and serving
