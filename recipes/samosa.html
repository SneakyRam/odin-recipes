SAMOSA




South Asian filled pastry

A samosa is a popular South Asian and African deep-fried or baked pastry with a crispy, flaky exterior and a savory, spiced filling, typically made from potatoes, peas, and onions, though it can also include meat or lentils


Ingredients:


  • Boiled potatoes
  • Green peas (fresh or frozen)
  • Cumin seeds
  • Coriander seeds (crushed)
  • Green chili (finely chopped)
  • Ginger (paste or grated)
  • Onion (chopped)
  • Turmeric powder
  • Chili powder
  • Garam masala
  • Salt
  • Coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Lemon juice (optional)
  • Oil for sautéing the filling

Steps:

  1. Mix flour, salt, and oil/ghee in a bowl until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  2. Gradually add water, kneading to form a stiff, non-sticky dough.
  3. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and sauté cumin seeds until they splutter.
  5. Add ginger, green chilies, and chopped onions, and sauté for a few minutes.
  6. Add the mashed or crumbled boiled potatoes, peas, and spices (turmeric, chili powder, garam masala, coriander, salt).
  7. Cook for a few minutes, mix well, and then add chopped coriander leaves and lemon juice. Set aside to cool.
  8. Assemble the Samosas
  9. Divide the rested dough into equal portions.
  10. Roll each portion into a thin circle and cut it in half.
  11. Moisten the straight edge of a half-circle with water and fold it into a cone shape.
  12. Spoon the potato filling into the cone, leaving space at the top to seal.
  13. Dampen the edges with water and press them together to seal the samosa completely.
  14. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai.
  15. Deep-fry the samosas on medium heat until they are golden brown and crispy.
  16. Serve hot.
